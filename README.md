# Tic-Tac-Toe
 
A Tic-Tac-Toe game played by two players 1 and 2 on a 3 x 3 grid. It is created by using Java. 

Definition:
Tic-tac-toe (American English), noughts and crosses (Commonwealth English), or Xs and Os is a paper-and-pencil game for two players, X and O, who take turns marking the spaces in a 3Ã—3 grid. The player who succeeds in placing three of their marks in a horizontal, vertical, or diagonal row is the winner.[1]

Rules:
- The player 1 places "X" characters and the player 2 places "O" characters.
- Players take turns placing characters into empty squares (" ").
- Player 1 "X" plays first
- "X" and "O" characters are always placed into empty squares, never on filled ones.
- The game ends when there are 3 of the same (non-empty) character filling any row, column, or diagonal.
- The game ends if all squares are non-empty which will be a draw.
